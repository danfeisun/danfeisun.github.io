Audio Movement

Audio Movement is about redesigning the public transit experience into a sanctuary for community involvement and self-improvement. With over 300,000 riders a day, 66% of whom use it for school or work, we found a need for a mobile office that offers them the resources that turn their commute time into productivity time. What Audio Movement offers, is a solution to enhance the lives of riders, gather data for the city, increase repeat traffic flow and boost funding opportunities for San Diego.

Research+Key Findings

To investigate the problem regarding low ridership on San Diego public transit, we interviewed riders on the bus and non-riders (at the mall and grocery store) as well as examined numerous “I Hate the Bus” Youtube vlogs and their comments to determine ubiquitous frustrations to be:

1.Smelly or inebriated strangers
2. Missing intended stops
3.Phone usage (everything from loud music/games to talking on the phone to “everyone looks like zombies on their phone”)
4. Negative stigma of bus riders being those who are low income and are riding the bus because they can not afford cars.

Challenge

How do we...

1. Create a space of inclusion in a confined, socioeconomically diverse place?
2. Personalize the bus riding experience to make it more enjoyable?
3. Remove the negative stigma of public transportation and their users?
4. Make sure riders don’t miss their stop when engaged in activities on the bus?.

Solution + Wireframes

Our solution is to help passengers on the MTS bus system turn their commute time into productivity time by providing access to an extensive media library (including Audible, TED, among others) at no charge, to better commuter experience and incentivize repeat traffic. Additionally, passengers have the option to pay as little as $1-2 extra for access to WiFi. By providing destination arrival alarms, passengers can focus on being productive without worrying about missing their stop. Our solution also gathers information on customer satisfaction through implementation of surveys and aims to brand San Diego as not only a smart city, but also a city of knowledge.

Final Deliverables

Hi-Fi Prototype, consists of destination arrival alarm to ensure one never misses their stop, an extensive media library improving commuting experience and incentivizing repeat traffic as well as community surveys to help understand commuters' needs and better customer outreach.

Personalized Stop Scheduling System

Start by entering a destination, we will recommend material based on its estimated arrival time. Riders will also be able to check our live map to see whether their buses are on time. There’s no need for them to worry about being too immersed in the experience and missing stops, we have implemented an alarm system that will alert the rider as well as their driver when their stop is near.

Turn Commute Time into Productivity Time

If they are in the mood to learn something new, they will be able to choose from our extensive database of Ted Talks, Podcasts, Lynda Tutorials and many other materials. They can also engage in their communities through learning about local news and events.

Incentivize Repeat Traffic Through Creating Accounts

We’re also in partnership with Audible, to provide an easily accessible learning experience. Riders will create an account to start free audiobooks, track and return to where they left off. Chosen from hundreds of thousands audiobook options, riders will have access to free audiobooks updated every month. Learning doesn’t have to stop at the end of their bus rides!

Community Involvement

To provide a better commuter experience, we have implemented surveys in our system for riders to voice their opinions on ways to ensure that MTS (Metropolitan Transit System) services are efficient and effective for San Diego's travel needs. The data will be accessible to MTS and SANDAG(San Diego Association of Governments) to better understand San Diegans’ needs in their daily commute as well as other aspects of their daily lives.
